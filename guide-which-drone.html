Which Drone Do I Need?

Different shots require different platforms. This guide helps you understand which drone type is right for your production, from corporate videos to Hollywood blockbusters.

Platform Breakdown

Understanding Drone Platforms

üöÅ

DJI Mavic Series

Entry Point for Professional Aerial

The Mavic series (Mavic 3 Pro, Mavic 3 Cine) offers excellent image quality in a compact, portable package. Perfect for productions that need professional results without the complexity and cost of larger platforms. The Hasselblad camera delivers stunning 5.1K footage with impressive dynamic range.

Sensor
4/3" CMOS
Max Resolution
5.1K / 50fps
Day Rate
$1,000 - $1,800

Best For

Real Estate Corporate Videos Events Travel Content Social Media Wedding Films News/Journalism

Not Ideal For

Feature Films High-End Commercials Cinema-Grade Color Heavy Wind Conditions
üé¨

DJI Inspire 3

The Industry Standard for Broadcast

The Inspire 3 is the workhorse of professional aerial cinematography. With a full-frame sensor, interchangeable lenses, and 8K ProRes RAW recording, it delivers broadcast and cinema-quality footage. The dual-operator capability allows separate control of flight and camera for precision shots.

Sensor
Full Frame
Max Resolution
8K / 25fps
Day Rate
$2,000 - $3,500

Best For

Broadcast Television Commercials Documentaries Music Videos Sports Coverage Automotive Premium Corporate

Not Ideal For

Tight Indoor Spaces Run-and-Gun Budget Productions
üé•

Heavy Lift Platforms

Cinema Cameras in the Sky

Heavy lift drones like the Freefly Alta X or custom builds can carry cinema cameras including RED Komodo, ARRI Alexa Mini, and Sony Venice. These platforms deliver identical image quality to your ground camera package, ensuring seamless integration in high-end productions.

Payload
Up to 15kg
Cameras
RED, ARRI, Sony
Day Rate
$3,500 - $8,000+

Best For

Feature Films Netflix/Streaming Originals Super Bowl Commercials Camera Matching Theatrical Release

Not Ideal For

Quick Turnaround Limited Budgets Tight Locations Run-and-Gun
‚ö°

FPV Drones

Immersive, Dynamic Movement

FPV (First Person View) drones create shots that were previously impossible. Flying through tight spaces, following action at high speed, and creating immersive one-take sequences. From cinematic 5" builds with GoPros to larger rigs carrying RED cameras, FPV has revolutionized action cinematography.

Speed
Up to 200+ km/h
Cameras
GoPro to RED
Day Rate
$2,500 - $5,000+

Best For

Action Sequences Car Chases Sports Music Videos One-Take Sequences Interior Fly-Throughs Extreme Sports

Not Ideal For

Static Establishing Shots Slow, Controlled Movement Long Flight Times
