Verification Methodology

How we ensure every record is legitimate and verified. Transparency and accuracy are our foundation.

Verification Requirements

GPS Data Requirements

  • Real-time RTK-GPS with accuracy to within 50cm recommended, or high-quality M9N/M8T receivers
  • Continuous logging throughout the entire flight with samples at minimum 10Hz
  • Format requirements: KML, GPX, or raw telemetry logs from autopilot system
  • Altitude verification required to ensure level flight or valid acceleration measurements

Video Evidence Standards

  • Multi-angle documentation recommended (chase camera, ground reference, onboard footage)
  • Clear speed readout visible during the record attempt from OSD or telemetry overlay
  • Continuous recording from takeoff through peak speed and landing
  • Minimum 1080p resolution at 30fps or higher frame rate

Witness Requirements

  • Independent observer who is not the pilot or immediate family member
  • Technical knowledge of drones or aviation recommended but not required
  • Signed statement documenting what was observed during the flight attempt
  • Contact information for verification team to follow up if necessary

Equipment Standards

All aircraft must meet the following minimum criteria for record consideration:

Aircraft Specifications

  • Complete specifications including motor configuration, battery type, propellers, and frame details
  • Weight documentation with proof of scale measurements
  • Legal compliance with local aviation regulations and airspace restrictions
  • Insurance highly recommended for international records

Measurement Standards

  • Speed measured in km/h, mph, or m/s â€” all will be converted to km/h for ranking
  • Multiple data sources compared for consistency (GPS vs. airspeed estimation)
  • Environmental factors documented (wind conditions, temperature, altitude)
  • Peak speed identification must be clearly visible in telemetry data

Verification Process

1

Initial Submission Review

Our team reviews all submitted documentation for completeness. We check that all required fields are filled and evidence files are accessible. If documentation is incomplete, we request additional information within 3 business days.

2

Data Analysis

GPS logs and telemetry are analyzed by our verification specialists. We cross-reference multiple data sources and look for any inconsistencies or anomalies. Video evidence is synchronized with telemetry data to confirm the recorded speed.

3

Witness Verification

We contact the independent witness to confirm their observations and validate their statement. We may request additional details or clarifications about the flight conditions and measurements.

4

Environmental Validation

We verify wind speeds, temperature, and altitude data from weather sources for the time and location of the attempt. These factors can significantly impact speed measurements and are important for context.

5

Approval & Publication

Once all checks pass, we notify the pilot of approval and publish the record on our leaderboard. A "Verified" badge is displayed with the record timestamp and verification date.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I submit a record without independent witnesses?
While independent witnesses are strongly recommended, we may consider submissions with multiple video angles and comprehensive GPS/telemetry data. However, these submissions will take longer to verify and have a lower approval probability without witness confirmation.
How long does verification take?
The typical verification timeline is 2-4 weeks. Complex cases involving international submissions or incomplete documentation may take longer. We provide email updates on submission status throughout the process.
What GPS receiver should I use?
RTK-GPS systems provide the highest accuracy. Recommended receivers include the Piksi Multi, M9N, or M8T modules. Standard GPS is acceptable but less reliable for precise speed measurements. If using standard GPS, ensure your system logs data at 10Hz or higher.
Can wind affect my speed measurement?
Yes, significantly. GPS measures ground speed while wind adds to airspeed. We document wind conditions for context, but records are ranked by ground speed (GPS-measured). Ideal conditions are calm winds for fastest GPS speeds. Always note actual wind speeds in your submission.
Can I submit historical records?
Yes! If you have complete documentation from a previous flight, you can submit it. However, records more than 12 months old may require additional verification. We recommend submitting records as soon as possible after the flight while witnesses and data are fresh.
What happens if my record is rejected?
We provide detailed feedback on why a record didn't pass verification. You're welcome to resubmit with corrected documentation. Many rejections are due to incomplete data â€” additional evidence often resolves the issue. Our team will work with you to address any concerns.
